Quiz Review Sheet

Quiz: DNA Barcoding

Ground rules/instructions

  1. No talking - During the quiz there is absolutely no talking. If you have a question, please raise your hand. If I can fairly answer your question (for example about a ambiguous statement or error) I will and will inform the other quiz takers.
  2. Spread out - you must have an empty chair to your right and left.
  3. You must bring your Chromebook - quiz will be online

Review

Here are the key items and study points you should review for the quiz:

  • You should be able to define and explain:

    • In your own words: What is DNA Barcoding
    • Give a few examples of why DNA Barcoding may be useful compared to a traditional approach to species identification (e.g. by using descriptive words, drawings, photos, etc.)
    • Know which genes are used for Animal/Plant barcoding

You should know what steps are involved in the following techniques, and what each step accomplishes in a protocol:

DNA Barcoding (Study slides and sample collection video) - Sample collection, DNA Extraction, DNA Sequencing, Sequence Comparison (DNA Subway/Bioinformatics)

DNA Extraction (Study protocol below) - Lysis buffer - Grinding of sample - Placing sample on filter paper - Washing of filter paper in wash buffer

DNA Subway

  • Know why reads must be trimmed
  • Know why reads must be reverse complimented
  • Know why we use a BLAST search

Helpful Resources

In addition to the notes and slides, study the following resources to prepare yourself for the quiz:

  • DNA Barcoding 101: Barcoding animation - Animations title
