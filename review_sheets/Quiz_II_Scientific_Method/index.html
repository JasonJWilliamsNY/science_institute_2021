Quiz Review Sheet

Quiz: Scientific Method and Bacterial Transformation Experiment

Ground rules/instructions

  1. No talking - During the quiz there is absolutely no talking. If you have a question, please raise your hand. If I can fairly answer your question (for example about a ambiguous statement or error) I will and will inform the other quiz takers.
  2. Spread out - you must have an empty chair to your right and left.
  3. You must bring your Chromebook - quiz will be online
  4. When the music stops you must submit your quiz - more info on this in class.

Review

Review slide decks I and II for the Bacterial transformation lab as well as our lab protocol. Also review the scientific method concept reading.

  • What is a model organism and why do we use model organisms in science?
  • What is a plasmid?
  • How are restriction enzymes used to create plasmids?
  • Know the major steps of our protocol and their purpose. For example:
  • Why are samples kept on ice?
  • Why do we add calcium chloride (CaCl2) to our samples
  • Why do we add the pGFP plasmid?
  • What two genes does the pGFP plasmid contain?
  • What is the Heat Shock method of bacterial transformation?
    • This animation on Heat Shock will be helpful.
  • Why do we plate on both LB and LB+Ampicillin plates?
  • What were the controls used in our experiment?
  • How would you define the scientific method?
