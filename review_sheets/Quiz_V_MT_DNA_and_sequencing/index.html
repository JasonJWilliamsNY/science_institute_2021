Quiz Review Sheet

Quiz: Mitochondrial DNA sequencing

Ground rules/instructions

  1. No talking - During the quiz there is absolutely no talking. If you have a question, please raise your hand. If I can fairly answer your question (for example about a ambiguous statement or error) I will and will inform the other quiz takers.
  2. Spread out - you must have an empty chair to your right and left.
  3. You must bring your Chromebook - quiz will be online
  4. When the music stops you must submit your quiz - more info on this in class.

Review

Here are the key items and study points you should review for the quiz:

  • You should be able to define/explain:

  • How DNA was isolated for mtDNA sequencing (e.g. saline rinse and chelex)

  • Distinct features of mtDNA (e.g. size, shape, inheritance)
  • What region of mtDNA was amplified and why
  • What are the components of a DNA sequencing reaction (e.g. PCR template,
    dNTPs, ddNTPs (labeled with dye), primer, polymerase, polyacrylamide gel)
  • What do we learn from an electropherogram/chromatogram (e.g. color of the peaks, height of the peaks)
  • What is a phred score
  • What is BLAST and what can it tell you

Helpful Resources

In addition to the notes and slides, study the following resources to prepare yourself for the quiz:

DNA sequencing animation: https://dnalc.cshl.edu/resources/animations/cycseq.html Mitochondrial DNA sequencing lab website: http://www.geneticorigins.org/mito/mitoframeset.htm Phred score: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phred_quality_score
