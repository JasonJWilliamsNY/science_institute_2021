04 Extraction of DNA from foods

This is a protocol outline for obtaining DNA from foods (processed or unprocessed).

Protocol

Materials

  • Microfuge tubes
  • Platic pestles
  • Edwards' buffer
  • Isopropanol
  • TE Buffer

Steps

DNA Extraction

  1. Obtain a very small amount of food product for sampling and add to a labeled tube.
  2. Dried items (e.g., potato chips, cereal) are good, but use small
    amounts since this will swell and form a gel/paste in the buffer.

  3. Fresh items (e.g., a lettuce leaf, piece of fruit) should easily generate DNA. It is still advisable to use a very small amount

  4. Add 100ul Edwards buffer to the tube with the food item. Then use a plastic pestle to grind and crush the food item (this should take about 60 seconds).

  5. After grinding, add and addition 900ul of Edwards buffer and carefully use the pestle to mix (without spilling the contents of the tube).

  6. Boil the samples for 5 minutes in a water bath.

  7. Centrifuge the samples for 2 minutes at maximum speed to separate the insoluble components from the supernatant (which should contain some usable DNA).

  8. Transfer 350ul of ground sample to a fresh tube. Avoid transferring any of the undissolved pellet (if necessary you can transfer less than 350ul - a cleaner sample is more important than the amount transferred.)

  9. Add 400ul isopropanol to the transferred supernatant and mix by inversion. Let this rest for 3 minutes.

  10. Centrifuge the supernatant/isopropanol tube for 5 minutes at maximum speed.

  11. Pour off the supernatant (leaving behind a DNA pellet). Spin the tube again for 30 seconds at maximum speed to collect any remaining alcohol. Use a pipette to remove as much of the isopropanol as possible.

  12. Air dry the pellet by leaving the tube open for 10 minutes.

  13. Add 100ul of TE buffer to the pellet to rehydrate. Mix and rehydrate for 5 minutes.

PCR

Each sample will be amplified using two sets of primers:

  • 35s Primer

  • Tubulin primer

  • Label the provided PCR tubes with master mix (one tube will have a 35s primer set, one will have a Tubulin primer set)

  • Add 2ul of the DNA to each of the two tubes.
